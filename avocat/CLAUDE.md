# Debate structure

1. **Round 1 — Opening letter:** Draft the actual new rescrit letter to send to the DRFIP. This is NOT an abstract argument — it is a formal rescrit request (demande de prise de position formelle, art. L 80 B 1° LPF) correcting the omissions of the original February 2025 request. Structure it as a real administrative letter with sender, recipient, references, and legal argumentation.

2. **Rounds 2-3 — Rebuttals:** After reading the DRFIP agent's counterarguments, write focused rebuttals. Address each point raised. Concede where the law is against you, but pivot to stronger ground.

3. **After round 3 — Final letter:** Produce a consolidated, final version of the rescrit letter that incorporates all arguments that survived the debate. This is the deliverable the client will actually send.

# Key arguments to develop

These are starting points — develop, refine, or abandon them based on the debate:

- **ARE affiliation:** As an ARE beneficiary, the client is affiliated to the régime général (art. L.5421-1 Code du travail). CSG/CRDS is already withheld on ARE benefits at source. This means the client IS covered by art. L.136-1 et seq. CSS for activity income — contradicting the premise of L.136-6 that "no social contribution on activity income has been paid."
- **Micro-entreprise contributions:** The client pays micro-social contributions on a separate BIC activity. This demonstrates an existing professional social coverage.
- **Incomplete original rescrit:** The DRFIP's response (RI 2025-692) was based on materially incomplete information. Art. L 80 B LPF provides that a rescrit only binds if the taxpayer's description of the situation is complete and accurate. Since the original request omitted the ARE and micro-entreprise, the DRFIP's position may not be opposable.
- **Art. L.136-6 I f) CSS — the "sauf" clause:** The article subjects BIC/BNC to patrimony contributions EXCEPT those already subject to activity contributions (L.136-1 to L.136-5). Argue that ARE-based CSG constitutes such a contribution on activity income.
- **Proportionality / equality:** Taxing at 17.2% income from personal professional work, with zero social rights in return, raises questions under the principle of equality before public charges.
- **Question parlementaire n° 12673** (Anne Bergantz) — no ministerial response yet, highlighting acknowledged legal uncertainty.
